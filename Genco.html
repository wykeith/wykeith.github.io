Electricity Pricing

Posted on Tue 09 October 2018 in Blog

What type of Power GenCo package is better: variable or fixed?¶

Link to Chart

Come 1st November, Singapore's retail electicity market will be liberalised. The electricity market will be a fully competitive market. Various power generating companies are now offering different packages to supply eletricity to the retail consumer. Honestly I think all the packages are confusing, with hidden costs like GST and Lost Transmission Factor these are not presented upfront to the consumer. Rather than comparing the indiviual pricing of all the packages which will be subject to change, I'll go through an anaylsis to make an informed choice of either a variable rate or fixed rate package.

It is worthwhile to note that if one were to stick to the incumbent SPPower , one would have paid addition premium as all other gencos are offering discounts off SP regulated tariff rates. Does this mean SP will be pressured to lower their domestic tariff rates causing a race condition for others to supply free electricity? Short answer is no as there are also business consumers which account for 80% of electricity demand.

The electricity tariff comprises two key components – fuel cost and non-fuel cost.

The fuel cost of 76.1% is tied to oil prices by commercial contracts, which change depending on global market conditions. The non-fuel cost is the cost of generating and delivering electricity to homes. Network cost forms a substantial part of this component at 22.0%

The 2 main cost components that determine the electrical tariff are the network cost and the fuel cost. Since the network cost is pretty much fixed upfront capex. The variability in tariff will be impacted by the fuel cost rather than the network cost.

Data.gov.sg publishs the fuel mix data for electricity generation. We observe that LNG accounts on average about 95% of the fuelmix. With this we shall study the LNG pricing closely.

A free source of LNG prices that can be downloaded from Quandl. There are from three major LNG futures exchanges in the world. Japan, US and Europe. Links to download them appended below

Since LNG pricing is recorded in USD , we should account for exchange rate flucation. The USDSGD exchange rates can be sourced from MAS' Exchange rate end point api

Observations¶

  • If the regulated tariffs rises above the upper band of the projected confidence internal, the discountd rates may never fall below the fixed rate of 16.88. Hence Genco X's fix rate is set fairly as it also touches the lower band of the projected confidence interval.
  • For cashflow budgeting certainty , one would go for a fixed rate package
  • If we believe that the regulated tariffs will fall inbetween the confidence intervals, there is a possibiliity that discounted rates may be cheaper than the fixed rate, one should go for a variable rate package instead. This is also assuming that electricity consumption is high enough to realise sizeable savings.
